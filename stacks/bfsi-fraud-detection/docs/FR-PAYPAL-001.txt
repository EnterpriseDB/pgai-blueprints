Fraud Detection Rule: FR-PAYPAL-001
Rule Name: France PayPal Compliance
Region: France (FR)
Payment Vendor: PayPal
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

OVERVIEW
This rule monitors PayPal transactions in France with focus on Bon Coin (leboncoin.fr) marketplace fraud, strict French consumer protection laws (Code de la consommation), cross-border EU transactions, and fraud patterns specific to the French market including sophisticated phishing and social engineering.

RULE PARAMETERS
- Threshold Amount: €3,500 EUR (approximately $3,800 USD)
- Risk Score Threshold: 0.75
- Action: Alert (flag for review with consumer protection verification)
- Category: Marketplace Fraud and Consumer Protection
- Regulatory Framework: ACPR (Autorité de Contrôle Prudentiel et de Résolution), Code de la consommation

RULE DESCRIPTION
French consumers benefit from strong consumer protection under the Code de la consommation, including 14-day cooling-off periods (droit de rétractation), extensive warranty rights, and strict merchant obligations. France also has very active online marketplaces (especially Leboncoin) creating unique fraud patterns. This rule addresses French-specific risks while ensuring regulatory compliance.

FRENCH-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

REGULATORY ENVIRONMENT:
- ACPR (banking and insurance regulator)
- DGCCRF (consumer protection authority)
- Code de la consommation (Consumer Code)
- Droit de rétractation (14-day withdrawal right for distance sales)
- Garantie légale de conformité (2-year legal warranty)
- Strong anti-fraud measures (Pharos platform)

MARKETPLACE LANDSCAPE:
- Leboncoin.fr (dominant classified ads platform)
- Vinted (second-hand clothing)
- eBay.fr
- Facebook Marketplace
- Marketplace fraud extremely common

PAYMENT CULTURE:
- Bank transfers (virement bancaire) very common
- Chèques (checks) still used (less common elsewhere)
- Carte Bancaire (CB) - French bank card system
- PayPal for online purchases and marketplaces
- Cash (espèces) for in-person marketplace transactions

LANGUAGE:
- French language required for consumer contracts
- Formal communication style
- Technical/legal precision important
- Strong enforcement of French language requirements

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

This rule triggers when:
1. PayPal transaction from French account ≥ €3,500
2. Marketplace transaction with elevated risk indicators
3. Fraud risk score ≥ 0.75
4. Pattern matching known French scam types

OR special conditions:
5. Leboncoin transaction over €1,000
6. "Amis et famille" (Friends and Family) for commercial transaction
7. Cross-border transaction with red flags
8. Vehicle purchase (high fraud category in France)
9. Rental deposit (especially Paris market)

FRAUD SCENARIOS SPECIFIC TO FRANCE

SCENARIO 1: ARNAQUE LEBONCOIN (LEBONCOIN SCAM)
Description: Fake seller scam on France's most popular classified ads platform.

Pattern:
- Attractive listing (vehicle, smartphone, apartment rental)
- Price below market value
- Seller claims urgency ("moving abroad", "need quick sale")
- Requests PayPal Amis et famille
- May send fake delivery tracking
- Item doesn't exist

Example:
- Listing: Renault Clio 2019, €8,500 (market value €12,000)
- Platform: Leboncoin
- Seller: "Déménage en Suisse, vente rapide nécessaire"
- Payment request: PayPal Amis et famille "pour éviter les frais"
- Red flags: Too cheap, urgency, no buyer protection
- Risk Score: 0.95
- Action: BLOCK, educate about Leboncoin fraud, recommend in-person cash only

SCENARIO 2: FAUSSE ANNONCE IMMOBILIÈRE (FAKE RENTAL LISTING)
Description: Fake apartment rental targeting people seeking housing in tight markets.

Paris Specificity:
- Extremely tight rental market in Paris
- Desperation creates vulnerability
- High deposits required (2-3 months rent)
- Common target for fraud

Pattern:
- Attractive Paris apartment at reasonable price
- "Owner" abroad, can't show apartment
- Requires deposit before viewing
- PayPal Amis et famille or bank transfer
- Apartment doesn't exist or isn't actually for rent

Example:
- Listing: 2-bedroom Marais apartment, €1,400/month
- Owner: "I'm in London, can't show personally"
- Deposit: €4,200 (3 months) via PayPal
- Red flags: Can't view, payment before seeing, urgency
- Risk Score: 0.92
- Action: BLOCK, warn about rental scams, recommend never paying before viewing

SCENARIO 3: ARNAQUE AU FAUX CONSEILLER (FAKE ADVISOR SCAM)
Description: Scammer impersonates bank advisor or official to request payment.

French Variation:
- Claims to be from customer's bank (Crédit Agricole, BNP Paribas, Société Générale)
- Or claims to be from "police fiscale" (tax police)
- Sophisticated French language
- Urgency and official tone
- Requests PayPal or bank transfer

Example:
- Call: "Bonjour, je suis conseiller à Crédit Agricole"
- Claim: "Suspicious activity on your account"
- Request: "Send €2,500 via PayPal to secure account"
- Red flags: Banks don't request PayPal payments, pressure tactics
- Risk Score: 0.94
- Action: BLOCK, educate about bank impersonation, report to Pharos (government anti-fraud platform)

SCENARIO 4: ARNAQUE À LA LIVRAISON (DELIVERY SCAM)
Description: Fake delivery notification leading to payment request.

Pattern:
- SMS or email claiming package awaiting delivery
- From fake "Colissimo" (La Poste), "Chronopost", or "DPD"
- Claims small customs fee or redelivery fee
- Links to fake payment page
- Steals payment info or charges large amount

Example:
- SMS: "Votre colis Colissimo attend. Frais de douane €2.50"
- Link: Fake La Poste website
- Requests: Full credit card or PayPal login
- Then: Large fraudulent charge (€500-€5,000)
- Risk Score: Account takeover + fraud
- Action: If fraud detected, BLOCK, notify customer, educate about delivery phishing

SCENARIO 5: ARNAQUE SENTIMENTALE (ROMANCE SCAM)
Description: Online romance leading to payment requests.

French Context:
- Targets lonely individuals on dating sites (Meetic, Tinder, etc.)
- "Partner" claims to be French person abroad
- Eventually needs emergency funds
- Exploits French romanticism and trust

Example:
- Customer: 65-year-old widow in Lyon
- Story: Met "French businessman" online
- Location: Claims working in Côte d'Ivoire
- Emergency: Needs €6,000 for medical emergency
- Red flags: Never met, emergency funds, foreign location
- Risk Score: 0.96
- Action: BLOCK, educate about romance scams, provide support resources, report to police

SCENARIO 6: FAUX SITE MARCHAND (FAKE E-COMMERCE SITE)
Description: Professional-looking fake French e-commerce site.

Pattern:
- Well-designed website in French
- Sells popular items at attractive prices
- Takes PayPal or credit card payment
- Never delivers or delivers counterfeit
- Site disappears after collecting payments

Example:
- Website: "electromenager-discount.fr"
- Products: Appliances at 30% discount
- Transaction: €2,200 for washing machine
- Red flags: Recently registered domain, no reviews, no physical address, prices too good
- Customer never receives item
- Risk Score: 0.87
- Action: HOLD transaction, verify merchant legitimacy, check domain age, verify physical address

SCENARIO 7: ARNAQUE AU TROP-PERÇU (OVERPAYMENT SCAM)
Description: Buyer "overpays" and requests refund via different method.

French Leboncoin Variation:
- Targets Leboncoin sellers
- Fake PayPal payment email
- Claims overpayment occurred
- Requests refund via bank transfer or cash
- Original payment never actually occurred

Example:
- Seller: Listing iPhone on Leboncoin for €800
- Buyer: Sends screenshot showing €1,200 payment
- Request: "Erreur, remboursez €400 par virement bancaire"
- Reality: No actual PayPal payment received
- Risk Score: 0.96
- Action: BLOCK any "refund", alert seller, educate about overpayment scams

SCENARIO 8: CRYPTO ARNAQUE (CRYPTOCURRENCY SCAM)
Description: Fake crypto investment or P2P crypto fraud.

Pattern:
- Customer wants to buy cryptocurrency
- Finds seller on Leboncoin, Facebook groups, or Telegram
- Agrees on amount (e.g., €5,000 for Bitcoin)
- Sends PayPal Amis et famille
- Never receives crypto
- Can't reverse payment

Example:
- Customer: Paris resident wants Bitcoin
- Platform: Leboncoin "cryptocurrency" section
- Amount: €7,500 for 0.15 BTC
- Payment: Amis et famille as requested
- Reality: Scammer disappears, no Bitcoin
- Risk Score: 0.90
- Action: BLOCK Amis et famille for crypto, educate, recommend licensed platforms only (Coinhouse, etc.)

FRENCH CONSUMER PROTECTION LAWS

DROIT DE RÉTRACTATION (RIGHT OF WITHDRAWAL):
14-day cooling-off period for distance sales:
- Customer can cancel without reason
- Full refund required within 14 days
- Customer returns goods at own expense (unless agreed otherwise)
- Exceptions: Perishable goods, personalized items, sealed media (if opened), accommodation

Merchant MUST:
- Inform customer of withdrawal right clearly
- Provide standard withdrawal form
- Process refund within 14 days of notification

GARANTIE LÉGALE DE CONFORMITÉ:
2-year legal warranty for goods:
- Product must conform to description
- Must be fit for purpose
- Defects within 24 months presumed to exist at purchase
- Buyer can request repair, replacement, or refund
- Stronger than many other countries

MENTIONS LÉGALES (LEGAL INFORMATION):
French law requires merchants to display:
- Company name and legal form
- Registered office address
- Company registration number (SIRET)
- VAT number if applicable
- Contact details
- Similar to German Impressum but specific French requirements

This rule verifies merchant compliance.

PRICE TRANSPARENCY:
- Total price including all taxes must be shown
- Delivery costs must be clear before order
- No hidden costs allowed
- Currency must be clear (EUR)

STRONG CUSTOMER AUTHENTICATION (SCA)

PSD2 IN FRANCE:
- SCA widely implemented
- 3D Secure 2.0 (3DS2) standard
- Many French banks use SMS OTP or mobile app
- Transactions >€30 typically require SCA

EXEMPTIONS:
- Low-risk transactions (fraud history dependent)
- Recurring payments after first SCA
- Trusted beneficiaries
- Low-value (<€30)

This rule monitors for SCA bypass attempts.

CROSS-BORDER EU TRANSACTIONS

FRENCH CROSS-BORDER COMMERCE:
- Significant trade with Germany, Belgium, Spain, Italy
- SEPA payments standard
- Language requirements (French descriptions for French customers)
- VAT rules for intra-EU sales
- Consumer protection extends to EU sellers targeting France

RISK FACTORS:
- First cross-border purchase over €2,000
- Non-EU merchants
- Merchants in high-fraud EU countries
- No French language terms/policies

AUTOMATED RESPONSE ACTIONS

WHEN SCORE 0.75-0.85 (Medium-High Risk):
1. Display strong warning in French
2. Offer to switch to "Biens et services" (Goods and Services)
3. Provide Leboncoin safety guidance
4. Require acknowledgment of risks
5. Proceed if customer confirms after warnings

WHEN SCORE 0.85-0.95 (High Risk):
1. HOLD transaction for manual review
2. Contact customer via verified phone
3. Ask targeted questions (in French)
4. Verify not responding to Leboncoin scam
5. Check seller legitimacy if marketplace transaction
6. Block if scam indicators clear

WHEN SCORE > 0.95 (Critical Risk):
1. BLOCK transaction immediately
2. Contact customer with fraud alert (French)
3. Provide specific scam education
4. Report to Pharos (government platform) if appropriate
5. Consider account review
6. Offer victim support resources

INTEGRATION WITH FRENCH AUTHORITIES

PHAROS (PLATEFORME D'HARMONISATION, D'ANALYSE, DE RECOUPEMENT ET D'ORIENTATION DES SIGNALEMENTS):
- Government platform for reporting internet fraud
- Managed by Ministry of Interior
- Victims can report scams online
- Intelligence sharing with police

DGCCRF (DIRECTION GÉNÉRALE DE LA CONCURRENCE, DE LA CONSOMMATION ET DE LA RÉPRESSION DES FRAUDES):
- Consumer protection authority
- Investigates merchant fraud
- Enforces Code de la consommation
- Can fine or prosecute non-compliant merchants

GENDARMERIE NATIONALE / POLICE NATIONALE:
- Cybercrime units (C3N for gendarmerie, BEFTI for police)
- Investigate fraud cases
- Coordinate with Europol for cross-border fraud

ACPR (AUTORITÉ DE CONTRÔLE PRUDENTIEL ET DE RÉSOLUTION):
- Banking and payment services regulator
- Oversees PayPal operations in France
- Consumer complaints handling

INFO ESCROQUERIES:
- Government hotline: 0 805 805 817
- Free information about scams
- Victim support
