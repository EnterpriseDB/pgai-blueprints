Fraud Detection Rule: US-SQUARE-001
Rule Name: US Square Merchant Risk Assessment
Region: United States (US)
Payment Vendor: Square
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

OVERVIEW
This rule monitors Square point-of-sale (POS) and online transactions for merchant-side fraud patterns, including friendly fraud, chargeback abuse, and suspicious merchant behavior that may indicate scams or unauthorized business practices.

RULE PARAMETERS
- Risk Score Threshold: 0.65
- Action: Review (merchant and transaction review)
- Category: Merchant Risk Assessment
- Focus: Both customer fraud AND merchant fraud

RULE DESCRIPTION
Square serves small businesses, pop-up shops, and individual sellers, which creates unique fraud risks. This rule addresses both traditional payment fraud and merchant-specific fraud patterns including:

1. Merchant impersonation and scams
2. Friendly fraud from customers
3. Chargeback manipulation
4. Card-not-present fraud for Square Online
5. Fake or unauthorized merchants

SQUARE-SPECIFIC RISK FACTORS

MERCHANT REPUTATION SIGNALS:
- New merchant account (< 90 days)
- High chargeback rate (> 1%)
- Customer complaints or disputes
- Inconsistent business description
- Website quality and legitimacy
- Social media presence verification
- Business registration verification

TRANSACTION PATTERNS:
- Sudden spike in transaction volume
- Unusually high average transaction amount
- Geographic mismatch (merchant location vs. customer)
- Off-hours transactions for typically daytime businesses
- Bulk purchases from single customer
- Gift card or prepaid card transactions

DEVICE AND HARDWARE:
- Square Reader serial number verification
- Device location changes (mobile readers moving unexpectedly)
- Multiple merchant accounts on single device
- Rooted or jailbroken device detection
- GPS location inconsistencies

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS
This rule triggers when fraud score reaches 0.65 or higher based on:

MERCHANT RISK SCORE (0-0.40):
- New merchant: +0.15
- High chargeback rate: +0.20
- No web presence: +0.10
- Unverified business: +0.15
- Category high-risk (electronics resale, jewelry): +0.10

TRANSACTION RISK SCORE (0-0.35):
- Amount > $500: +0.10
- Card-not-present: +0.10
- International card: +0.10
- Multiple attempts: +0.15

CUSTOMER RISK SCORE (0-0.25):
- New customer: +0.10
- Multiple failed cards: +0.15
- VPN/proxy: +0.10

Total Score = Merchant + Transaction + Customer scores

FRAUD SCENARIOS

SCENARIO 1: Fake Merchant Scam
Description: Criminal sets up fake Square merchant account to process fraudulent transactions, then disappears before chargebacks arrive.

Indicators:
- Brand new Square account
- No physical business location
- High-value transactions immediately
- Multiple different customer cards
- Merchant unresponsive to Square communication
- Funds withdrawn immediately

Example:
- Merchant: "Premium Electronics Outlet"
- Account age: 3 days
- Transaction: $2,800 laptop sale
- Customer: First-time, using card from different state
- Red flag: No website, no business registration, temporary phone number
- Risk Score: 0.85 → HOLD transaction, verify merchant

SCENARIO 2: Friendly Fraud (Customer-Initiated)
Description: Customer makes legitimate purchase but later disputes charge claiming fraud or non-receipt.

Indicators:
- Customer has history of chargebacks
- High-value item (electronics, jewelry)
- Pickup or immediate delivery
- Customer pressures for quick transaction
- No signature or proper documentation

Example:
- Customer purchases $1,500 camera equipment
- Pays with credit card via Square Terminal
- Two weeks later files chargeback: "unauthorized transaction"
- Evidence shows customer was present (signature, ID checked)
- Risk Score: 0.70 → Document everything, prepare chargeback defense

SCENARIO 3: Chargeback Ring
Description: Organized fraud where multiple "customers" purchase from complicit merchant, then all file chargebacks after merchant withdraws funds and disappears.

Indicators:
- Multiple transactions in short time from different cards
- All transactions similar amounts
- Cards from various geographic locations
- Merchant withdraws funds immediately
- Pattern repeats across multiple merchant accounts

Example:
- Merchant: "Wholesale Gift Cards"
- 15 transactions of $800-$1,200 over 2 days
- All cards from different states
- Merchant transfers funds to external account
- Next week: 15 chargebacks filed
- Risk Score: 0.90 → FREEZE merchant account, investigate

SCENARIO 4: Card Testing via Square
Description: Criminals test stolen card numbers using Square merchants (often with merchant cooperation).

Indicators:
- Many small transactions ($1-$5)
- Sequential card numbers or patterns
- Failed transactions followed by successes
- Different billing/shipping info
- Merchant in high-risk category

Example:
- Coffee shop processes 50 transactions of $1-$2 in one hour
- All different cards
- Customer never actually present
- Same IP address for all transactions (Square Online)
- Risk Score: 0.95 → BLOCK merchant, report to law enforcement

SQUARE POS VS. SQUARE ONLINE

Square POS (Card-Present) Considerations:
- Lower fraud risk generally (chip card verification)
- Focus on friendly fraud and merchant scams
- Device location verification important
- Customer signature and receipt documentation
- Physical goods exchange verification

Square Online (Card-Not-Present) Considerations:
- Higher fraud risk (no physical card verification)
- CVV and AVS checks critical
- Shipping address verification
- Digital goods have highest risk
- Email and phone verification recommended

AUTOMATED RESPONSE ACTIONS

WHEN SCORE 0.65-0.75 (Medium Risk):
1. Flag transaction for review
2. Require additional documentation
3. Hold funds for extended period (7 days vs. standard 2 days)
4. Request customer signature and ID
5. Send merchant fraud prevention tips

WHEN SCORE 0.75-0.85 (High Risk):
1. Hold transaction for manual approval
2. Contact merchant to verify transaction
3. Request proof of goods/service delivery
4. Extend chargeback reserve period
5. Consider reducing processing limits

WHEN SCORE >0.85 (Critical Risk):
1. Block transaction immediately
2. Freeze merchant account
3. Escalate to Square fraud team
4. Request comprehensive documentation
5. Possible merchant account suspension
6. Law enforcement notification if criminal activity suspected

CHARGEBACK DEFENSE

Square sellers must maintain:
- Transaction receipts and signatures
- Proof of delivery (tracking numbers, signatures)
- Customer communication records
- Product/service descriptions
- Photos of items sold (for high-value transactions)
- Return/refund policy acknowledgment

This rule automatically flags transactions for enhanced documentation when fraud risk is elevated, helping merchants defend against illegitimate chargebacks.

EXAMPLES OF LEGITIMATE HIGH-RISK TRANSACTIONS

Example 1 - Art Fair Vendor:
- Merchant: Jewelry artist at weekend art fair
- Transaction: $850 custom necklace
- Risk factors: New customer, high value, mobile POS
- Legitimacy signals: Event verification, customer present, item photographed
- Score: 0.62 → APPROVE with enhanced documentation

Example 2 - Home Repair Service:
- Merchant: Licensed contractor
- Transaction: $3,200 down payment for renovation
- Risk factors: Large amount, service industry
- Legitimacy signals: License verified, customer called to confirm, detailed invoice
- Score: 0.58 → APPROVE, standard processing

Example 3 - Pop-Up Retail:
- Merchant: Sneaker reseller at pop-up shop
- Transaction: $600 limited edition sneakers
- Risk factors: Temporary location, high-value shoes, resale category
- Legitimacy signals: Social media following verified, products authentic, customer satisfied
- Score: 0.68 → APPROVE with 7-day hold and documentation

COMPLIANCE CONSIDERATIONS

SQUARE SELLER AGREEMENT:
- Merchants agree to maintain chargeback rate < 1%
- Excessive chargebacks may result in account termination
- Prohibited business types (adult, gambling without license, etc.)
- Accurate business description required

PAYMENT CARD INDUSTRY (PCI DSS):
- Square handles PCI compliance for merchants
- Merchants must still follow best practices
- Card data should never be stored by merchant
- Square encryption protects card data

STATE REGULATIONS:
- Money transmitter licenses (varies by state)
- Sales tax collection compliance
- Business registration requirements
- Professional licenses for certain services

PERFORMANCE METRICS
Target metrics for this rule:
- Chargeback Prevention Rate: > 70%
- False Positive Rate: < 15% (higher tolerance for merchant protection)
- Merchant Satisfaction: Minimal disruption to legitimate business
- Fraud Loss Reduction: > 60% compared to no monitoring
- Response Time: < 2 hours for merchant communication

SEASONAL AND EVENT ADJUSTMENTS

HOLIDAY SHOPPING (Nov-Dec):
- Increased transaction volumes expected
- Higher average transaction amounts normal
- Gift purchases common (different shipping addresses okay)
- Return fraud monitoring increased in January

FESTIVALS AND MARKETS:
- Temporary merchant locations expected
- Mobile reader location changes normal
- Cash-equivalent sales (tickets, gift cards) increase
- Verify event credentials and permits

TAX SEASON (April):
- Service providers see payment spikes
- Higher single-transaction amounts
- Professional services (accountants, lawyers)
- Verify business legitimacy for new providers

This rule adapts thresholds based on merchant category and calendar seasonality to reduce false positives while maintaining fraud detection effectiveness.
