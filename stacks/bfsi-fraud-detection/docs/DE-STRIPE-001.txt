Fraud Detection Rule: DE-STRIPE-001
Rule Name: Germany SEPA Fraud Detection
Region: Germany (DE)
Payment Vendor: Stripe
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

OVERVIEW
This rule monitors Stripe transactions in Germany with focus on SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area) fraud, strong consumer protection compliance (Widerrufsrecht), cross-border EU transactions, and fraud patterns specific to the German market including sophisticated phishing and social engineering attacks.

RULE PARAMETERS
- Threshold Amount: €4,000 EUR (approximately $4,300 USD)
- Risk Score Threshold: 0.70
- Action: Review (mandatory review with consumer protection verification)
- Category: Transaction Limit and Consumer Protection
- Regulatory Framework: BaFin supervision, German Civil Code (BGB), EU directives

RULE DESCRIPTION
German consumers benefit from some of the world's strongest consumer protection laws, including mandatory cooling-off periods (Widerrufsrecht), extensive chargeback rights, and strict merchant obligations. However, sophisticated fraud operations also target Germany due to its high GDP and widespread digital payment adoption. This rule balances fraud prevention with German regulatory compliance.

GERMAN-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

REGULATORY ENVIRONMENT:
- BaFin (Bundesanstalt für Finanzdienstleistungsaufsicht) - Financial regulator
- German Civil Code (Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch - BGB)
- Widerrufsrecht - 14-day right of withdrawal for distance sales
- Impressum requirement - merchant transparency obligation
- Payment Services Supervision Act (Zahlungsdiensteaufsichtsgesetz - ZAG)
- Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) under PSD2

CONSUMER PROTECTION LAWS:
- Fernabsatzgesetz (Distance Selling Act) - merged into BGB
- 14-day cooling-off period mandatory for online purchases
- Right to return goods without reason
- Clear cancellation policy required
- Refund processing within 14 days
- Extensive information obligations for merchants

SEPA CONSIDERATIONS:
- Single Euro Payments Area includes Germany
- Direct debit (SEPA Lastschrift) very common in Germany
- IBAN-based payments standard
- SEPA Direct Debit fraud patterns
- Mandate management critical

LANGUAGE AND FORMALITY:
- German language required for consumer contracts
- Formal "Sie" vs informal "Du" matters in communications
- Technical accuracy important (Germans value precision)
- Clear, detailed explanations expected
- Impressum (imprint) legally required on merchant websites

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

This rule triggers when:
1. Stripe transaction from German account ≥ €4,000
2. Transaction involves German card, SEPA payment, or German merchant
3. Fraud risk score ≥ 0.70
4. Consumer protection compliance concerns

OR special conditions:
5. SEPA Direct Debit with elevated risk
6. First high-value transaction (≥ €2,000) on new account
7. Cross-border EU transaction with red flags
8. Missing consumer protection requirements (Impressum, Widerrufsrecht)
9. Merchant non-compliance with German regulations

RISK SCORE CALCULATION

BASE GERMAN RISK: 0.15
Lower baseline due to strong regulatory environment and lower overall fraud rates

MERCHANT COMPLIANCE (+0.00 to +0.40):
- No Impressum on website: +0.35 (legal violation)
- Missing cancellation policy: +0.30
- No clear Widerrufsrecht information: +0.25
- Not in German language: +0.20
- Unclear pricing/total cost: +0.15
- Suspicious merchant (new, no reviews): +0.30

TRANSACTION CHARACTERISTICS (+0.00 to +0.30):
- Amount significantly above customer history: +0.25
- High-risk product category: +0.20
- Digital goods (limited Widerrufsrecht): +0.15
- Cross-border to non-EU: +0.25
- SEPA Direct Debit from new mandate: +0.20

CUSTOMER INDICATORS (+0.00 to +0.30):
- New account (< 60 days): +0.20
- No transaction history: +0.15
- Recent account changes (password, email, IBAN): +0.20
- Multiple failed payment attempts: +0.20
- Unverified German address: +0.15

TIMING AND BEHAVIOR (+0.00 to +0.20):
- Unusual hour (Germans typically don't shop late night): +0.10
- Multiple rapid transactions: +0.20
- VPN/proxy from outside Germany: +0.20
- New device never seen: +0.15

Example Score:
- Base risk: 0.15
- No Impressum on merchant site: +0.35
- Amount 5x customer average: +0.25
- New account: +0.20
- VPN detected: +0.20
- Total: 1.15 → BLOCK TRANSACTION

FRAUD SCENARIOS SPECIFIC TO GERMANY

SCENARIO 1: FINANZAMT (TAX OFFICE) SCAM
Description: Scammers impersonate German tax authorities requesting payment.

Pattern:
- Email or call from fake "Finanzamt"
- Claims of unpaid taxes or tax refund
- Requests immediate payment via credit card
- Threatens legal action, fines, or arrest
- May use official-looking documents

Example:
- Customer: Berlin resident
- Contact: Email from "finanzamt-berlin@[fake-domain].de"
- Claim: €3,500 in back taxes owed
- Threat: "Legal proceedings within 48 hours"
- Payment: Stripe payment to fake entity
- Red flags: Real Finanzamt uses SEPA, not credit cards; sends physical mail first
- Risk Score: 0.95
- Action: BLOCK, educate about Finanzamt procedures, report to LKA (Landeskriminalamt)

SCENARIO 2: FAKE ONLINE SHOP (FAKE-SHOP)
Description: Sophisticated fake e-commerce sites targeting German consumers.

German Specificity:
- Well-designed German-language sites
- Appear to comply with German law (fake Impressum)
- Products at attractive prices
- Takes payment via Stripe
- Never delivers goods or delivers counterfeits

Example:
- Website: "elektronik-discount24.de"
- Products: iPhones, MacBooks at 20% discount
- Transaction: €2,800 for MacBook Pro
- Red flags: Recently registered domain, no real Impressum (fake address), no consumer reviews
- Customer never receives item or receives counterfeit
- Risk Score: 0.85
- Action: HOLD transaction, verify merchant legitimacy, check domain age, verify Impressum

SCENARIO 3: SEPA LASTSCHRIFT (DIRECT DEBIT) FRAUD
Description: Fraudulent SEPA Direct Debit mandates or unauthorized debits.

Pattern:
- Customer signs up for "free trial"
- Provides IBAN for "verification"
- Unexpectedly charged via SEPA Direct Debit
- Or completely fraudulent mandate (stolen IBAN)
- Recurring charges difficult to stop

Example:
- Customer: Hamburg resident
- Sign-up: "Free trial" for streaming service
- Reality: €89.99 charged monthly via SEPA
- Difficult cancellation process
- Risk Score: 0.75
- Action: Review mandate legitimacy, verify customer consent, ensure clear cancellation options

SCENARIO 4: PHISHING - "PAKET" (PACKAGE) SCAM
Description: Fake package delivery notifications leading to payment requests.

German Variation:
- SMS or email: "Ihr Paket von DHL/Hermes wartet"
- Claims customs fee or failed delivery
- Links to fake payment page
- Requests credit card payment via Stripe
- May install malware

Example:
- Customer receives SMS: "Paket liegt zur Abholung bereit. Gebühr €2.50"
- Link to fake DHL site
- Site requests full credit card details
- Then attempts large purchase (€500-€5,000)
- Risk Score: Account takeover risk + fraudulent charges
- Action: If fraudulent transaction detected, BLOCK, notify customer, educate about phishing

SCENARIO 5: ENERGY CRISIS SCAM (ENERGIE-PREISANPASSUNG)
Description: Scammers exploit energy price volatility to request payments.

Context:
- German energy prices highly volatile
- Confusion about government subsidies
- Scammers impersonate energy companies

Pattern:
- Email from fake energy provider
- Claims immediate payment needed for "Preisanpassung" (price adjustment)
- Or fake government energy subsidy application requiring fee
- Requests credit card payment

Example:
- Email: "Vattenfall Dringend: Energiepreisanpassung"
- Claim: €1,200 immediate payment required
- Link: Fake payment page
- Red flags: Real energy companies use SEPA, send physical letters
- Risk Score: 0.88
- Action: BLOCK, educate about energy company procedures, report to Verbraucherzentrale

SCENARIO 6: INVESTMENT SCAM (KAPITALANLAGE-BETRUG)
Description: Fake investment platforms promising high returns.

German Angle:
- Targets Germans' savings culture
- Professional-looking platforms
- Claims of "BaFin-regulated" (false)
- Crypto, forex, or binary options trading
- Initial small "returns" shown
- Large investment requested, then disappears

Example:
- Platform: "GermanCryptoInvest"
- Claims: "BaFin-lizenziert, 15% monatliche Rendite"
- Transaction: €8,000 investment
- Red flags: BaFin registration fake, guaranteed returns impossible
- Risk Score: 0.92
- Action: BLOCK, verify with BaFin registry, educate about investment fraud

SCENARIO 7: ROMANTIC SCAM - "MILITÄR IM AUSLAND"
Description: Romance scam claiming to be German soldier abroad.

Pattern:
- Meets victim on dating site
- Claims to be Bundeswehr (German military) stationed abroad
- Develops relationship online
- Eventually needs emergency funds
- Requests payment via Stripe

Example:
- Customer: 58-year-old woman in Munich
- Story: "Bundeswehr officer" in Mali peacekeeping
- Emergency: Needs €5,000 for medical treatment
- Red flags: Never met, military storyline, emergency funds
- Risk Score: 0.94
- Action: BLOCK, educate about romance scams, provide support resources

CONSUMER PROTECTION COMPLIANCE

WIDERRUFSRECHT (RIGHT OF WITHDRAWAL):
14-day cooling-off period for distance sales:
- Customer can cancel without reason
- Full refund required within 14 days
- Customer returns goods at own expense (unless agreed otherwise)
- Exceptions: Perishable goods, customized items, sealed media (if opened)

Merchant MUST provide:
- Clear cancellation policy in German
- Sample cancellation form (Muster-Widerrufsformular)
- Information about cancellation deadline
- Return address

This rule verifies:
- Merchant website includes proper Widerrufsrecht information
- Cancellation process is clear
- German language documentation provided

IMPRESSUM REQUIREMENT:
German law requires website imprint with:
- Company name and legal form
- Complete address (P.O. box not sufficient)
- Contact details (phone, email)
- Commercial register number
- VAT ID
- Responsible person's name

This rule checks:
- Impressum exists and is easily accessible
- Information appears legitimate (not fake address)
- Company can be verified in commercial register
- Red flag if missing or suspicious

PRICE TRANSPARENCY:
- Total price including VAT must be shown
- Shipping costs must be clear before checkout
- No hidden costs allowed
- Currency must be clear (EUR)

SEPA DIRECT DEBIT MANDATES:
- Customer must explicitly authorize (signed mandate)
- Pre-notification required (14 days advance for first debit, 2 days for recurring)
- Mandate reference number must be provided
- Customer can reverse debit within 8 weeks (13 months if unauthorized)

STRONG CUSTOMER AUTHENTICATION (SCA)

PSD2 REQUIREMENTS IN GERMANY:
Germans adopted SCA widely:
- 3D Secure 2.0 standard
- Biometric authentication common
- SMS OTP or app-based authentication
- Transactions >€30 typically require SCA

EXEMPTIONS:
- Low-risk transactions (fraud rate dependent)
- Recurring payments after first SCA
- Trusted beneficiaries (whitelist)
- Low-value transactions (<€30)

This rule monitors:
- SCA bypass attempts
- Failed SCA leading to suspicious retry patterns
- New mandates without proper authentication

CROSS-BORDER EU TRANSACTIONS

INTRA-EU COMMERCE:
- Germany is largest EU economy
- Significant trade with Netherlands, France, Poland, Italy
- SEPA payments standard across EU
- VAT rules for cross-border B2C sales
- Consumer protection laws apply to EU merchants selling to Germans

RISK FACTORS:
- Non-EU merchants (outside SEPA area)
- Merchants in high-fraud EU countries
- First cross-border purchase over €2,000
- Merchant doesn't display German language/terms

VERIFICATION:
- Check merchant EU VAT registration (VIES database)
- Verify merchant legitimacy in home country
- Confirm consumer protection compliance
- Check for German-language terms (required if targeting Germans)

AUTOMATED RESPONSE ACTIONS

WHEN SCORE 0.70-0.80 (Medium-High Risk):
1. Flag for review by German fraud team
2. Verify merchant Impressum and compliance
3. Check consumer protection information
4. Confirm customer understanding
5. Provide Widerrufsrecht reminder
6. Proceed if verification satisfactory

WHEN SCORE 0.80-0.90 (High Risk):
1. HOLD transaction for manual approval
2. Contact customer via verified phone
3. Enhanced merchant verification
4. Check BaFin registry if investment-related
5. Verify SEPA mandate if applicable
6. Block if compliance issues or customer unresponsive

WHEN SCORE > 0.90 (Critical Risk):
1. BLOCK transaction immediately
2. Contact customer with fraud warning (in German)
3. Provide specific scam education
4. Report to LKA or Bundeskriminalamt if appropriate
5. Consider account review
6. Notify BaFin if investment fraud
