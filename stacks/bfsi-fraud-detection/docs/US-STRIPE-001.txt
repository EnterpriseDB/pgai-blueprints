Fraud Detection Rule: US-STRIPE-001
Rule Name: US High-Value Transaction Limit
Region: United States (US)
Payment Vendor: Stripe
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

OVERVIEW
This rule monitors high-value transactions processed through Stripe in the United States to detect potential fraud. Transactions exceeding the threshold amount require additional review.

RULE PARAMETERS
- Threshold Amount: $5,000.00 USD
- Risk Score Threshold: 0.70
- Action: Review (manual or automated review required)
- Category: Transaction Limit

RULE DESCRIPTION
Any single transaction processed through Stripe originating from a US-based customer or merchant that exceeds $5,000 must undergo enhanced fraud detection screening. This includes:

1. Customer verification - Confirm customer identity and account standing
2. Transaction history analysis - Review past transaction patterns
3. Merchant verification - Validate merchant legitimacy and category
4. Geographic consistency - Verify transaction location matches customer profile

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS
A transaction triggers this rule when ALL of the following are true:
- Payment vendor is Stripe
- Transaction region is United States
- Transaction amount is greater than or equal to $5,000.00
- Calculated fraud score is 0.70 or higher

FRAUD INDICATORS
- Sudden increase in transaction amount compared to customer history
- First-time high-value transaction from new customer
- Multiple failed payment attempts before successful transaction
- Mismatched billing and shipping addresses
- Use of anonymizing services or VPN connections
- High-risk merchant category (crypto, gambling, gift cards)

REVIEW PROCESS
When this rule is triggered:
1. Transaction is held for review (not automatically declined)
2. Customer may be contacted for verification
3. Additional authentication may be required
4. Review must be completed within 2 hours during business hours
5. Escalate to senior fraud analyst if fraud score exceeds 0.85

EXAMPLES

Example 1 - Legitimate Transaction:
- Customer: Established account, 3 years history
- Amount: $5,200 purchase of electronics from Best Buy
- Pattern: Consistent with previous high-value purchases
- Result: Approve after quick review

Example 2 - Suspicious Transaction:
- Customer: New account, created 2 days ago
- Amount: $8,500 purchase from "Crypto Exchange XYZ"
- Pattern: First transaction, no history
- IP: Using VPN, location mismatch
- Result: Hold for enhanced verification, contact customer

Example 3 - Fraudulent Transaction:
- Customer: Account shows signs of takeover
- Amount: $6,000 gift card purchase
- Pattern: Unusual for customer profile
- Failed attempts: 3 declined cards before success
- Result: Block transaction, freeze account, contact legitimate owner

COMPLIANCE NOTES
This rule helps comply with:
- Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) requirements
- FinCEN reporting obligations for suspicious activity
- Stripe's terms of service for fraud prevention
- PCI DSS requirements for transaction monitoring

PERFORMANCE METRICS
Target metrics for this rule:
- False Positive Rate: < 5%
- Detection Rate: > 90% for actual fraud
- Average Review Time: < 30 minutes
- Customer Impact: Minimal friction for legitimate customers
