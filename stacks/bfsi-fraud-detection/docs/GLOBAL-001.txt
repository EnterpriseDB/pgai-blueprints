Fraud Detection Rule: GLOBAL-001
Rule Name: Global High-Value Transaction Review
Region: GLOBAL (All Regions)
Payment Vendor: All Vendors
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

OVERVIEW
This is a universal rule that applies across all regions and payment vendors to detect and review exceptionally high-value transactions that may indicate fraud, money laundering, or other financial crimes. This rule serves as a final safety net regardless of regional rules.

RULE PARAMETERS
- Threshold Amount: $10,000.00 USD (or equivalent in local currency)
- Risk Score Threshold: 0.50
- Action: Review (mandatory human review)
- Category: HITL Threshold (Human-in-the-Loop)
- Review SLA: 1 hour during business hours, 4 hours outside business hours

RULE DESCRIPTION
Any transaction exceeding $10,000 USD requires mandatory human review before processing, regardless of other risk indicators. This rule ensures that large financial transactions receive appropriate scrutiny and helps meet regulatory requirements for anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) compliance.

CURRENCY CONVERSION
Amounts in foreign currencies are converted to USD at real-time exchange rates for threshold comparison:
- €10,000 EUR ≈ $10,800 USD → Triggers review
- £10,000 GBP ≈ $12,500 USD → Triggers review
- $15,000 CAD ≈ $11,000 USD → Triggers review
- ¥1,500,000 JPY ≈ $10,100 USD → Triggers review

Exchange rates updated hourly from reliable financial data sources.

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS
Transaction triggers this rule when ALL of the following are true:
1. Transaction amount ≥ $10,000 USD (or equivalent)
2. Calculated fraud score ≥ 0.50 (moderate risk or higher)
3. Transaction not on pre-approved high-value customer list

REVIEW REQUIREMENTS

TIER 1 REVIEW ($10,000 - $24,999):
- Verify customer identity and account status
- Review transaction history and patterns
- Confirm merchant legitimacy
- Check for geographic consistency
- Validate payment method and source of funds
- Review time: 15-30 minutes

TIER 2 REVIEW ($25,000 - $49,999):
- All Tier 1 requirements PLUS:
- Senior fraud analyst approval required
- Enhanced due diligence on customer
- Source of funds verification
- Beneficial owner identification
- Review time: 30-60 minutes

TIER 3 REVIEW ($50,000+):
- All Tier 1 and 2 requirements PLUS:
- Fraud manager approval required
- Possible customer interview
- Bank reference verification
- Consider filing Currency Transaction Report (CTR) if applicable
- Legal/compliance team notification
- Review time: 1-4 hours

FRAUD RISK FACTORS
Higher scrutiny applied when combined with:

HIGH-RISK INDICATORS:
- New customer account (< 30 days old)
- First transaction on account
- International transaction to high-risk country
- High-risk merchant category (crypto, gambling, money services)
- Unusual for customer's profile or income level
- Multiple payment methods used
- Rushed delivery or pickup request
- Customer reluctant to provide information

MEDIUM-RISK INDICATORS:
- Customer has transaction history but amount is significantly higher than usual
- Cross-border transaction to moderate-risk country
- Business hours transaction from business account
- Merchant in retail or professional services
- Customer responsive and provides documentation

LOW-RISK INDICATORS:
- Established customer with long history
- Transaction consistent with customer profile
- Domestic transaction
- Reputable merchant
- Standard delivery method
- Customer has high credit limit or verified income

EXAMPLES

Example 1 - Suspicious High-Value Transaction (BLOCK):
- Amount: $15,000 purchase of cryptocurrency
- Customer: Account created 5 days ago
- History: No previous transactions
- Payment: Multiple credit cards used
- Location: VPN/proxy detected
- Merchant: Unlicensed crypto exchange
- Customer contact: Unresponsive to verification request

Risk Score: 0.95
Action: BLOCK transaction, freeze account, file Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)

Example 2 - Legitimate Business Transaction (APPROVE):
- Amount: $18,500 purchase of office equipment
- Customer: Established business, 4 years history
- History: Regular purchases of $5,000-$10,000
- Payment: Business credit card
- Location: Company headquarters address
- Merchant: Authorized Dell/HP reseller
- Documentation: Purchase order provided

Risk Score: 0.35 (below 0.50 threshold, but still reviewed due to amount)
Action: APPROVE after 20-minute review

Example 3 - Wire Fraud Attempt (BLOCK):
- Amount: $25,000 wire transfer
- Customer: Senior citizen, retired teacher
- Trigger: Customer contacted claiming "IRS debt" needs immediate payment
- Pattern: Highly unusual for customer
- Red flags: Urgency, threat of arrest, unusual beneficiary
- Merchant: Unknown money transfer service

Risk Score: 0.88
Action: BLOCK immediately, contact customer, report to authorities

Example 4 - Luxury Purchase (APPROVE with verification):
- Amount: $12,000 designer handbag purchase
- Customer: High-income professional, verified salary $250K+
- History: Previous luxury purchases of $3,000-$8,000
- Payment: Personal credit card with $50K limit
- Location: Customer's home address in Beverly Hills
- Merchant: Legitimate luxury retailer (Neiman Marcus)

Risk Score: 0.52
Action: Quick verification call to customer, APPROVE

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

BANK SECRECY ACT (BSA):
- Transactions ≥ $10,000 may require Currency Transaction Report (CTR)
- File CTR within 15 days of transaction
- Multiple related transactions (structuring) also reportable

SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORT (SAR):
- File SAR if transaction appears suspicious regardless of amount
- SAR deadline: 30 days from detection
- Do not notify customer that SAR was filed

KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC):
- Enhanced due diligence for high-value customers
- Verify identity, address, source of funds
- Ongoing monitoring requirements

ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML):
- Screen against OFAC and sanctions lists
- Identify politically exposed persons (PEPs)
- Monitor for layering and integration patterns

OFFICE OF FOREIGN ASSETS CONTROL (OFAC):
- Screen transactions against SDN list
- Block transactions to sanctioned countries/entities
- Report blocked transactions within 10 days

INTERNATIONAL CONSIDERATIONS

HIGH-RISK COUNTRIES (Enhanced scrutiny):
- Countries on FATF blacklist or greylist
- Sanctions-restricted countries
- Known money laundering havens
- High corruption index countries

SPECIAL RULES BY REGION:
- European Union: GDPR compliance for data handling
- United Kingdom: FCA regulations, £10,000 threshold
- Canada: FINTRAC reporting requirements
- Australia: AUSTRAC transaction reporting

CROSS-BORDER TRANSACTIONS:
- Additional documentation required
- Purpose of transaction verification
- Relationship between sender and recipient
- Expected future transaction patterns

INTEGRATION WITH OTHER SYSTEMS

VELOCITY CHECKING:
- Multiple high-value transactions in short time period
- Aggregate transactions approaching regulatory thresholds
- Pattern of transactions just below reporting limits (structuring)

CUSTOMER RISK RATING:
- Low-risk customers: Simplified review process
- Medium-risk: Standard review procedures
- High-risk: Enhanced due diligence required
- Adjust risk rating based on transaction patterns

MERCHANT MONITORING:
- Merchants with high chargeback rates get additional scrutiny
- Newly registered merchants flagged for review
- Merchant category code (MCC) risk assessment

ALLOWLISTING

PRE-APPROVED HIGH-VALUE CUSTOMERS:
Customers may be allowlisted for expedited review when they:
- Have 2+ years of transaction history
- Regularly process high-value transactions
- Maintain excellent account standing
- Provide enhanced verification (income documentation, bank references)
- Accept ongoing monitoring requirements

Allowlisted customers still require review but with shorter SLA (15 minutes vs. 1 hour).

PERFORMANCE METRICS
Target operational metrics:
- Legitimate Transaction Approval Rate: > 95%
- False Positive Rate: < 10%
- Review Time (Tier 1): < 30 minutes average
- Customer Satisfaction: Minimal friction for legitimate customers
- Fraud Detection Rate: > 85% for actual fraud
- Compliance Rate: 100% of required reports filed on time

ESCALATION PROCEDURES

IMMEDIATE ESCALATION to Fraud Manager:
- Amount exceeds $100,000
- Suspected money laundering or terrorist financing
- Politically exposed person (PEP) involved
- Customer threatens legal action
- Media or reputational risk

ESCALATION to Legal/Compliance:
- Regulatory inquiry or subpoena
- Pattern suggests organized fraud ring
- Cross-border sanctions concerns
- Unusual transaction structure suggesting tax evasion

LAW ENFORCEMENT CONTACT:
- Active fraud in progress
- Threats of violence
- Suspected human trafficking or illegal goods
- Elder abuse or vulnerable victim

CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION

Email Notification Template:
"Thank you for your purchase of $[AMOUNT]. To ensure your security, we need to verify this transaction. Please call us at [PHONE] or click [LINK] to complete verification. Your transaction will be processed once verification is complete. This typically takes 15-30 minutes."

Phone Script for Verification:
"Hello [NAME], this is [ANALYST] from [COMPANY] fraud prevention. We noticed a transaction of $[AMOUNT] on your account. For your security, we need to verify some information. This will only take a few minutes..."

Questions to Ask:
1. Did you authorize this transaction?
2. Can you describe what you purchased?
3. Have you purchased from this merchant before?
4. What is the source of funds for this purchase?
5. Are you under any pressure to make this payment?

TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
Analysts reviewing high-value transactions must complete:
- AML/BSA compliance training (annual)
- SAR filing procedures (annual)
- Customer interview techniques (semi-annual)
- Cultural sensitivity training (for international transactions)
- Fraud typology updates (quarterly)

AUDIT AND DOCUMENTATION
Every high-value transaction review must document:
- Reviewer name and ID
- Date and time of review
- Risk factors identified
- Customer contact attempts and responses
- Documentation reviewed
- Approval/denial reasoning
- Manager approval for Tier 2/3 transactions
- Regulatory filings made (CTR, SAR, etc.)

Documentation retained for minimum 5 years per regulatory requirements.
