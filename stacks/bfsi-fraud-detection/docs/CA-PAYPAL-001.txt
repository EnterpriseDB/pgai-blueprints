Fraud Detection Rule: CA-PAYPAL-001
Rule Name: Canada PayPal Fraud Prevention
Region: Canada (CA)
Payment Vendor: PayPal
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

OVERVIEW
This rule monitors PayPal transactions in Canada with focus on e-transfer scams, online marketplace fraud, cross-border shopping complications, and unique Canadian fraud patterns affecting PayPal users.

RULE PARAMETERS
- Threshold Amount: CAD $3,000 (approximately USD $2,200)
- Risk Score Threshold: 0.75
- Action: Alert (flag for review with educational intervention)
- Category: Fraud Prevention and Customer Protection

RULE DESCRIPTION
Canadian PayPal users face specific challenges including the prevalence of Interac e-Transfer as an alternative payment method (creating confusion), active online marketplace fraud (Kijiji, Facebook Marketplace), package forwarding schemes, and bilingual scam campaigns. This rule addresses these Canadian-specific risks.

CANADIAN PAYPAL LANDSCAPE

PAYMENT ECOSYSTEM:
- Interac e-Transfer dominates P2P payments (competes with PayPal)
- Confusion between Interac and PayPal
- PayPal less common for P2P than in US
- Common for online marketplace purchases
- Cross-border shopping with US merchants
- Cryptocurrency purchases via PayPal

INTERAC VS PAYPAL CONFUSION:
- Scammers exploit confusion between the two
- "Send via Interac or PayPal" - creates ambiguity
- No buyer protection on Interac (major difference)
- Victims may think protections are equivalent
- Educational intervention critical

MARKETPLACE LANDSCAPE:
- Kijiji (most popular classifieds)
- Facebook Marketplace
- Craigslist (less popular than US)
- VarageSale (local community sales)
- eBay Canada

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

This rule triggers when:
1. PayPal transaction from Canadian account ≥ CAD $3,000
2. Marketplace transaction with risk indicators
3. Fraud risk score ≥ 0.75
4. Pattern matching known Canadian scam types

OR special conditions:
5. "Friends and Family" payment for goods/services
6. First marketplace transaction over CAD $1,000
7. Cross-border payment with red flags
8. Interac-related phishing indicators
9. Package forwarding risk factors

FRAUD SCENARIOS SPECIFIC TO CANADIAN PAYPAL

SCENARIO 1: KIJIJI/FACEBOOK MARKETPLACE SCAM
Description: Fake seller scam on Canadian online marketplaces.

Pattern:
- Attractive listing (vehicle, electronics, furniture)
- Price below market value
- Seller claims "moving soon" or "need quick sale"
- Requests PayPal Friends and Family
- May provide fake tracking number
- Item doesn't exist or not as described

Example:
- Listing: 2020 Honda Civic, CAD $12,000 (market value $18,000)
- Platform: Kijiji Toronto
- Seller: "Relocating to Vancouver, must sell this week"
- Payment request: PayPal Friends and Family "to avoid fees"
- Red flags: Too cheap, pressure, no protection
- Risk Score: 0.92
- Action: BLOCK, educate about marketplace fraud, recommend Goods and Services only

SCENARIO 2: "INTERAC NOT WORKING" SCAM
Description: Seller claims Interac e-Transfer isn't working, requests PayPal instead.

Canadian Context:
- Interac is default for P2P in Canada
- Scammers exploit preference by claiming technical issues
- Pushes victims to less familiar PayPal
- Specifically requests Friends and Family

Example:
- Customer: Buying used iPhone in Calgary for CAD $800
- Seller: "My Interac isn't working, can you send PayPal?"
- Requests: Friends and Family to "avoid delays"
- Red flags: Interac rarely has issues, F&F request, no buyer protection
- Risk Score: 0.82
- Action: ALERT customer, recommend in-person cash transaction or Goods and Services

SCENARIO 3: OVERPAYMENT / FAKE PAYMENT SCAM
Description: Buyer "overpays" and requests refund of difference.

Canadian Variation:
- Targets sellers on Kijiji, Facebook Marketplace
- Fake PayPal payment email (looks official)
- Asks for refund via Interac e-Transfer
- Original PayPal payment never actually occurred
- Seller out the "refund" amount

Example:
- Seller: Listing laptop for CAD $1,200 on Facebook Marketplace
- Buyer: Sends screenshot showing CAD $1,600 payment
- Request: "Please refund $400 via Interac, my mistake"
- Reality: No actual PayPal payment received
- Risk Score: 0.95
- Action: BLOCK any "refund", alert seller, educate about overpayment scams

SCENARIO 4: RENTAL/DEPOSIT SCAM
Description: Fake rental listing requires deposit via PayPal.

Canadian Angle:
- Targets tight rental markets (Toronto, Vancouver)
- Lists fake apartment/room rental
- Can't show property (out of town, traveling)
- Requires deposit via PayPal Friends and Family
- Property doesn't exist or isn't actually for rent

Example:
- Listing: Downtown Toronto apartment, CAD $1,800/month
- Landlord: "I'm in Calgary, can't show in person"
- Deposit: First and last month = CAD $3,600
- Payment: Must be Friends and Family for "tax reasons"
- Red flags: Can't view, F&F required, pressure to commit
- Risk Score: 0.90
- Action: BLOCK, warn about rental scams, recommend viewing before payment

SCENARIO 5: PACKAGE FORWARDING / RESHIPPING SCAM
Description: Scammer uses victim's PayPal to purchase goods, ships to victim, asks victim to forward.

Pattern:
- Victim offered "work from home" job
- "Job" is receiving packages and reshipping
- Packages purchased with stolen PayPal accounts
- Victim becomes unwitting accomplice to fraud
- Victim may be liable for stolen goods

Example:
- Customer: Mississauga resident, work-from-home "job"
- Activity: Receiving electronics, reshipping to US/overseas
- Payment: "Employer" sends money via PayPal
- Reality: Packages bought with stolen accounts, customer is mule
- Risk Score: 0.85
- Action: BLOCK payments, educate about reshipping scams, report to RCMP if confirmed

SCENARIO 6: QUEBEC FRENCH-LANGUAGE PHISHING
Description: Phishing emails in French targeting Quebec PayPal users.

Pattern:
- Well-written French phishing email
- Claims account issue or suspicious activity
- Requests "verification" via link
- Fake PayPal login page
- Steals credentials and financial info

Example:
- Target: Quebec PayPal users
- Email: "PayPal: Activité suspecte détectée"
- Content: "Veuillez vérifier votre compte immédiatement"
- Link: Fake PayPal site (paypa1.com or similar)
- Risk Score: Account takeover risk
- Action: If account accessed from fake site, freeze account, force password reset, educate about phishing

SCENARIO 7: ROMANCE SCAM WITH PAYPAL
Description: Online romance leading to PayPal payment requests.

Canadian Context:
- Targets lonely/isolated Canadians
- "Partner" claims to be Canadian abroad
- Eventually needs emergency funds
- May claim to need funds to return to Canada
- Requests via PayPal Friends and Family

Example:
- Customer: 58-year-old in Thunder Bay, Ontario
- Story: Met "Canadian soldier" on dating site
- Stationed "overseas on UN mission"
- Emergency: Needs CAD $5,000 for medical treatment
- Red flags: Never met, emergency funds, Friends and Family
- Risk Score: 0.95
- Action: BLOCK, educate about romance scams, provide support resources, report to CAFC

SCENARIO 8: CRYPTO PURCHASE SCAM
Description: Fake Canadian crypto platforms or P2P crypto scams.

Pattern:
- Customer wants to buy cryptocurrency
- Finds "seller" on Kijiji, Facebook, Localbitcoins
- Agrees on amount (e.g., CAD $4,000 for Bitcoin)
- Sends PayPal Friends and Family payment
- Never receives crypto
- Can't reverse F&F payment

Example:
- Customer: Vancouver crypto buyer
- Platform: Localbitcoins or Facebook group
- Amount: CAD $5,500 for Bitcoin
- Payment: Friends and Family as requested
- Reality: Scammer disappears, no Bitcoin delivered
- Risk Score: 0.88
- Action: BLOCK F&F for crypto, educate about crypto scams, recommend registered exchanges only

QUEBEC-SPECIFIC PATTERNS:

- Desjardins credential phishing (major Quebec financial institution)
- Cultural references in scams (Quebec politics, events)

CROSS-BORDER (CANADA-US) CONSIDERATIONS

LEGITIMATE PATTERNS:
- Shopping from US merchants (cheaper prices, more selection)
- PayPal standard for US e-commerce
- Package forwarding to Canada
- Family in both countries
- Snowbirds with US addresses

RED FLAGS:
- Package forwarding to residential address
- First-time high-value US purchase
- Merchant unknown or suspicious
- Multiple US shipping addresses
- Can't explain reason for US purchase

CURRENCY CONFUSION:
- USD vs CAD confusion
- Exchange rate not understood
- Unexpected total amount
- Merchant displays USD but customer thinks CAD
- PayPal conversion fees not anticipated

EDUCATIONAL INTERVENTIONS:

When customer selects Friends and Family for marketplace purchase:

"⚠️ WARNING: PayPal Friends and Family provides NO buyer protection.

You selected Friends and Family, but this appears to be a purchase of goods or services. Friends and Family should only be used for:
✓ Sending money to family members
✓ Reimbursing friends
✓ Gifts to people you know personally

If you're purchasing something:
✓ Use Goods and Services for buyer protection
✓ If seller insists on Friends and Family, it may be a scam
✓ You will NOT be able to dispute or get a refund

Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre: 1-888-495-8501

Do you want to change to Goods and Services payment? [YES / NO]"

MARKETPLACE SAFETY GUIDANCE:

"Tips for Safe Marketplace Transactions in Canada:

BEST PRACTICES:
✓ Meet in public place (police station parking lot ideal)
✓ Inspect item before paying
✓ Use cash for in-person transactions
✓ If using PayPal, always use Goods and Services
✓ Never pay via Friends and Family for purchases
✓ Be wary of deals that seem too good to be true

RED FLAGS:
✗ Seller insists on Friends and Family
✗ Can't meet in person / item is "shipped"
✗ Price significantly below market value
✗ Pressure to pay quickly
✗ Seller's story doesn't add up
✗ Requests Interac deposit to "hold" item

For more information: antifraudcentre-centreantifraude.ca"

AUTOMATED RESPONSE ACTIONS

WHEN SCORE 0.75-0.85 (Medium-High Risk):
1. Display strong warning about payment type
2. Offer to switch to Goods and Services
3. Provide marketplace safety tips
4. Require customer acknowledgment of risks
5. Proceed if customer confirms after warnings

WHEN SCORE 0.85-0.95 (High Risk):
1. HOLD transaction for manual review
2. Contact customer via verified phone
3. Ask targeted questions about transaction
4. Verify not responding to scam
5. Block if customer shows scam victim indicators

WHEN SCORE > 0.95 (Critical Risk):
1. BLOCK transaction immediately
2. Contact customer with fraud alert
3. Provide specific scam education
4. Report to CAFC if appropriate
5. Consider account review
6. Offer resources and support
