Fraud Detection Rule: UK-PAYPAL-001
Rule Name: UK PayPal Cross-Border Detection
Region: United Kingdom (UK)
Payment Vendor: PayPal
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

OVERVIEW
This rule monitors PayPal transactions originating from the UK with special focus on cross-border transactions, post-Brexit payment complications, and fraud patterns specific to UK PayPal users. The rule addresses both domestic UK fraud and international transaction risks.

RULE PARAMETERS
- Threshold Amount: £2,500 GBP (approximately $3,100 USD)
- Risk Score Threshold: 0.70
- Action: Alert (flag for review with possible hold)
- Category: Cross-Border Transaction Monitoring
- Post-Brexit Focus: EU transactions now treated as international

RULE DESCRIPTION
UK PayPal users face unique challenges and risks following Brexit, including currency conversion issues, customs complications, and increased fraud targeting cross-border transactions. This rule provides specialized monitoring for these scenarios while maintaining protection against traditional PayPal fraud.

UK-SPECIFIC PAYPAL CONSIDERATIONS

POST-BREXIT CHANGES:
- UK-EU transactions now cross-border (previously domestic EEA)
- Additional customs declarations required
- VAT and duty implications
- Currency conversion (GBP to EUR) more common
- Longer delivery times may be normal
- "EU Exit" customs charges affecting buyers

PAYPAL UK REGULATIONS:
- FCA regulation as e-money institution
- Payment Services Regulations 2017 compliance
- Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) under PSD2
- Consumer protection under UK law
- Chargeback rights and PayPal Buyer Protection

CURRENCY HANDLING:
- Primary currency: GBP (£)
- Secondary: EUR for EU transactions, USD for US
- Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) monitoring
- Multi-currency accounts common among UK users
- Exchange rate dispute prevention

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

This rule triggers when:
1. PayPal transaction from UK account ≥ £2,500
2. Cross-border transaction with elevated risk indicators
3. Fraud risk score ≥ 0.70
4. Post-Brexit complications present

OR any of these special conditions:
5. First international transaction over £1,000
6. Rapid sequence of international payments
7. Transaction to high-risk country
8. Significant currency conversion involved
9. Customs/VAT red flags

FRAUD SCENARIOS SPECIFIC TO UK PAYPAL

SCENARIO 1: BREXIT-RELATED PURCHASE SCAM
Description: Scammers exploit confusion around post-Brexit customs and VAT charges.

Pattern:
- Customer orders from EU seller
- Receives unexpected customs/VAT charge
- Scammer impersonates customs/courier
- Requests additional payment via PayPal
- Parcel is fake or charge is fraudulent

Example:
- Customer: UK buyer, purchased item from Germany
- Scam: Email claiming £150 customs charge must be paid
- Red flags: PayPal payment to individual (not official customs), pressure tactics
- Risk Score: 0.85
- Action: BLOCK payment, educate customer about legitimate customs procedures, report phishing

SCENARIO 2: CURRENCY CONVERSION FRAUD
Description: Merchant manipulates currency conversion to overcharge customer.

Pattern:
- Transaction in foreign currency (EUR or USD)
- Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) applied
- Unfavorable exchange rate (sometimes 5-10% worse)
- Customer doesn't realize they're being overcharged
- Or scammer adds hidden currency conversion fees

Example:
- Transaction: £2,000 purchase from EU merchant
- Conversion: Merchant charges €2,500 (poor rate)
- Real rate: Should be €2,350
- Loss: £130 to customer via poor conversion
- Risk Score: 0.65
- Action: ALERT customer to currency conversion issue, suggest disputing charges

SCENARIO 3: FAKE EU SELLER / NON-DELIVERY
Description: Scammer poses as EU seller, takes payment, never delivers goods.

Post-Brexit Angle:
- Longer delivery times expected (customs delays)
- Buyer waits longer before suspecting fraud
- Scammer uses "Brexit delays" as excuse
- By the time fraud is clear, dispute window may be closing

Example:
- Customer: Orders luxury handbag from "Paris boutique"
- Payment: £3,200 via PayPal goods and services
- Seller: Provides fake tracking number
- Reality: No such boutique exists, no item shipped
- Delay tactic: "Customs hold-up due to Brexit"
- Risk Score: 0.78
- Action: Review merchant legitimacy, check for other complaints, hold payment if suspicious

SCENARIO 4: OVERPAYMENT SCAM (UK VARIATION)
Description: Scammer "accidentally" overpays and requests refund difference.

UK Angle:
- Often targets UK sellers on eBay, Gumtree, Facebook Marketplace
- Fake PayPal payment notification email
- Payment is fraudulent but appears real
- Seller refunds "overpayment" from their own funds
- Original payment bounces/reverses

Example:
- UK Seller: Listing laptop for £800
- Scammer: Sends £1,200, claims accident
- Request: Refund £400 difference
- Reality: Original £1,200 payment is fraudulent
- Risk Score: 0.90
- Action: BLOCK refund transaction, alert seller, investigate original payment

SCENARIO 5: AUTHORISED PUSH PAYMENT VIA PAYPAL
Description: Victim tricked into sending money via PayPal (UK-specific fraud type).

Common Stories:
- HMRC tax scam (owe back taxes)
- Bank impersonation (move money to safe account)
- Romance scam (online partner needs emergency funds)
- Investment opportunity (send deposit)
- Family member emergency (grandchild arrested, needs bail)

Example:
- Customer: 68-year-old from Birmingham
- Call: Claims to be from HMRC
- Claim: £5,500 owed in back taxes, immediate payment needed
- Request: Send via PayPal to avoid prosecution
- Red flags: Urgency, government agency requesting PayPal, threats
- Risk Score: 0.95
- Action: BLOCK transaction, contact customer, educate about HMRC scams, report to Action Fraud

SCENARIO 6: HOLIDAY ACCOMMODATION SCAM
Description: Fake holiday rentals targeting UK travelers post-pandemic.

Pattern:
- Listing on legitimate site or social media
- Price too good to be true
- Requests payment via PayPal "Friends and Family" (no protection)
- Property doesn't exist or isn't actually for rent
- Common for EU holidays, especially Spain, France, Italy

Example:
- Customer: Family booking villa in Spain
- Listing: £3,000 for week in July
- Payment method: PayPal Friends and Family requested
- Red flags: Won't accept goods/services payment, no reviews, pressure to book
- Risk Score: 0.82
- Action: ALERT customer, recommend goods/services payment only, verify property legitimacy

CROSS-BORDER TRANSACTION VERIFICATION

HIGH-RISK DESTINATION COUNTRIES:
Transactions to these countries get additional scrutiny:
- Nigeria, Ghana (419 scams, overpayment scams)
- Russia, Ukraine (current geopolitical risks)
- China (counterfeit goods, non-delivery)
- Romania, Bulgaria (card fraud, phishing)
- India, Pakistan (tech support scams, overpayment)

MEDIUM-RISK:
- Any country outside UK/EU/US/Canada/Australia
- Countries with weak consumer protection laws
- Jurisdictions with poor PayPal dispute resolution

LOW-RISK:
- UK domestic transactions
- US, Canada, Australia
- Western European countries (despite Brexit)
- Japan, South Korea, Singapore

VERIFICATION PROCEDURES:

For high-risk international transactions:
1. Verify merchant legitimacy (website, reviews, registration)
2. Confirm shipping address is valid and deliverable
3. Check for merchant history with PayPal
4. Review customer's explanation for purchase
5. Verify customer understands import duties/VAT
6. Ensure proper buyer protection in place
7. Document customs/tax expectations

PAYPAL BUYER PROTECTION COMPLIANCE

UK LAW INTEGRATION:
- Consumer Rights Act 2015
- Consumer Credit Act 1974 (Section 75)
- Distance Selling Regulations
- PayPal Buyer Protection overlays these

BUYER PROTECTION COVERS:
- Item not received
- Item significantly not as described
- Unauthorized transaction
- Eligible items and transactions

NOT COVERED:
- Friends and Family payments
- Vehicles, real estate
- Custom-made items (sometimes)
- Digital goods (limited coverage)
- Industrial machinery

This rule ensures proper buyer protection is in place for eligible transactions and alerts customers when protection doesn't apply.

BREXIT-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE

CUSTOMS AND VAT CHANGES:
Post-Brexit rules for UK-EU transactions:
- Imports over £135: VAT and customs duty may apply
- Customs declarations required
- Longer delivery times normal
- "EU Exit" security charges common
- Some couriers charge handling fees

EDUCATIONAL ALERTS:
When UK customer makes EU purchase:
"Important: Post-Brexit, this EU purchase may be subject to UK customs charges and VAT upon delivery. These are separate from your PayPal payment and collected by the courier. Typical charges are 20% VAT plus any applicable customs duty. For more information, visit: gov.uk/goods-sent-from-abroad"

MERCHANT REQUIREMENTS:
EU merchants selling to UK must:
- Register for UK VAT if threshold exceeded
- Include VAT in price or disclose it clearly
- Provide accurate customs information
- Declare value honestly (underdeclaring is illegal)

STRONG CUSTOMER AUTHENTICATION (SCA)

PSD2 REQUIREMENTS:
PayPal UK must implement SCA for:
- Remote payments over £30
- First transaction with new merchant
- Adding new payment method
- Changing payment details

SCA METHODS:
- PayPal app push notification
- SMS one-time code
- Biometric (fingerprint, face ID)
- Security key

EXEMPTIONS:
- Low-risk transactions (determined by PayPal)
- Recurring payments/subscriptions
- Trusted beneficiaries
- Low-value (under £30)

This rule monitors for SCA bypass attempts and ensures compliance.

AUTOMATED RESPONSE ACTIONS

WHEN SCORE 0.70-0.80 (Medium-High Risk):
1. Send alert to fraud team for review
2. Display educational content to customer about cross-border risks
3. Verify shipping address is legitimate
4. Confirm customer understands import charges
5. Ensure buyer protection applies
6. Review merchant reputation

WHEN SCORE 0.80-0.90 (High Risk):
1. HOLD transaction for manual approval
2. Contact customer to verify intent
3. Enhanced merchant verification
4. Check for similar scam reports
5. Evaluate if "Friends and Family" being misused
6. Consider blocking if customer unresponsive

WHEN SCORE > 0.90 (Critical Risk):
1. BLOCK transaction immediately
2. Contact customer with fraud warning
3. Provide scam education materials
4. Report to PayPal fraud team
5. Consider account review if repeated high-risk attempts
6. File report with Action Fraud if appropriate
