Fraud Detection Rule: GLOBAL-002
Rule Name: Global Crypto Exchange Monitoring
Region: GLOBAL (All Regions)
Payment Vendor: All Vendors
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

OVERVIEW
This rule provides specialized monitoring for cryptocurrency exchange transactions, which present unique fraud risks including money laundering, scams, regulatory violations, and high volatility-related fraud. Given the global and decentralized nature of crypto, this rule applies universally across all regions and payment vendors.

RULE PARAMETERS
- Threshold Amount: $1,000.00 USD (or equivalent)
- Risk Score Threshold: 0.80
- Action: Review (mandatory human review with enhanced due diligence)
- Category: High-Risk Merchant Category
- Regulatory Focus: Anti-Money Laundering (AML), Know Your Customer (KYC), Counter-Terrorism Financing (CTF)

RULE DESCRIPTION
Cryptocurrency transactions require heightened scrutiny due to:
1. High fraud rates in crypto industry
2. Money laundering and terrorist financing risks
3. Regulatory uncertainty and compliance requirements
4. Scams targeting inexperienced investors
5. Irreversible nature of crypto transactions
6. Volatile market conditions enabling price manipulation
7. International sanctions evasion potential

WHY CRYPTO REQUIRES SPECIAL MONITORING

UNIQUE RISK FACTORS:
- Transactions are irreversible (cannot chargeback crypto)
- Pseudo-anonymous nature complicates identification
- Cross-border by default (no geographic boundaries)
- Regulatory requirements vary dramatically by jurisdiction
- High-value targets for hackers and social engineering
- Pump-and-dump schemes and market manipulation
- Initial Coin Offering (ICO) scams
- Fake exchanges and wallet services
- Romance scams ending in crypto payment requests

REGULATORY LANDSCAPE:
- United States: FinCEN Money Services Business (MSB) registration required
- European Union: 5th Anti-Money Laundering Directive (5AMLD)
- United Kingdom: FCA registration mandatory since January 2020
- Canada: FINTRAC registration for Money Service Businesses
- Many countries: Outright bans or severe restrictions

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

This rule triggers when ALL of the following are true:
1. Merchant is identified as cryptocurrency exchange or crypto service
2. Transaction amount ≥ $1,000 USD equivalent
3. Calculated fraud risk score ≥ 0.80

MERCHANT IDENTIFICATION:
Crypto exchanges identified by:
- Merchant Category Code (MCC): 6051 (Crypto currency)
- Merchant name contains: "crypto", "bitcoin", "exchange", "blockchain", "coin"
- Known exchange domain/business name
- Self-reported category: digital currency

RISK SCORE CALCULATION

BASE CRYPTO RISK: 0.50
All crypto transactions start with elevated baseline risk

ADDITIONAL RISK FACTORS:

MERCHANT VERIFICATION (+0.00 to +0.30):
- Unlicensed exchange in regulated jurisdiction: +0.30
- No regulatory registration: +0.25
- New exchange (< 1 year): +0.20
- Poor online reputation/reviews: +0.15
- No customer support: +0.10
- Established, licensed exchange (Coinbase, Kraken): +0.05

CUSTOMER PROFILE (+0.00 to +0.20):
- First crypto purchase: +0.15
- Elderly customer (> 65 years): +0.20 (high scam risk)
- Recent account takeover indicators: +0.20
- History of scam victimization: +0.15
- No prior crypto knowledge/experience: +0.10

TRANSACTION PATTERN (+0.00 to +0.20):
- Urgent or pressured transaction: +0.20
- Multiple failed payment attempts: +0.15
- Amount unusual for customer: +0.15
- Round number large amount ($10K, $25K): +0.10
- Outside customer's normal transaction times: +0.10

COMMUNICATION RED FLAGS (+0.00 to +0.10):
- Customer mentions "investment opportunity": +0.10
- Romance scam keywords ("fiancé needs help"): +0.10
- Claims of guaranteed returns: +0.10
- Pressure from unknown third party: +0.10

Example Score Calculation:
- Base crypto risk: 0.50
- Unlicensed exchange: +0.30
- First crypto purchase: +0.15
- Urgent transaction: +0.20
- Total: 1.15 → HIGH RISK, BLOCK TRANSACTION

COMMON CRYPTO FRAUD SCENARIOS

SCENARIO 1: ROMANCE SCAM WITH CRYPTO PAYMENT
Description: Victim develops online relationship, "partner" eventually requests crypto investment or emergency funds.

Pattern:
- Customer has no crypto history
- Often elderly or isolated individual
- Large single transaction ($5K-$50K)
- Sends to unfamiliar exchange or wallet
- May mention: "help my family", "investment together", "can't access their funds"

Example:
- Customer: 67-year-old widow, Margaret from Ohio
- Transaction: $23,000 to unknown crypto exchange
- Contact: Met "military officer" on dating site 3 months ago
- Red flags: Urgency, first crypto transaction, unusual amount
- Risk Score: 0.95
- Action: BLOCK transaction, contact customer, educate about romance scams, report to FBI IC3

SCENARIO 2: FAKE CRYPTO INVESTMENT PLATFORM
Description: Scammers create fake investment platforms promising high returns on crypto investments.

Pattern:
- Fake website looks legitimate (copied from real exchanges)
- Promises guaranteed returns (10-20% monthly)
- Shows fake account balances and "profits"
- Requests increasingly large "investments"
- Eventually unable to withdraw funds

Example:
- Customer: Young professional, excited about crypto
- Transaction: $15,000 to "BitMax Pro" exchange
- Website: Professional-looking but created 2 months ago
- Red flags: Promises 15% monthly returns, no regulatory registration
- Risk Score: 0.90
- Action: BLOCK, warn customer, report domain to authorities

SCENARIO 3: CRYPTO MONEY LAUNDERING
Description: Criminals convert proceeds of crime into cryptocurrency to obscure origin.

Pattern:
- Multiple small transactions (structuring)
- Aggregating just below reporting thresholds
- Use of privacy coins (Monero, Zcash)
- Chain of exchanges to obscure trail
- Rapid conversion back to fiat currency

Example:
- Customer: Business account with vague description
- Pattern: Daily $9,500 purchases of crypto (just under $10K reporting)
- Frequency: 5 days per week for 3 weeks
- Red flags: Structuring, no legitimate business purpose
- Risk Score: 0.88
- Action: BLOCK, file Suspicious Activity Report (SAR), freeze account

SCENARIO 4: EXCHANGE HACK / INSIDER THEFT
Description: Customer account at legitimate exchange compromised, funds stolen.

Pattern:
- Sudden large withdrawal after account compromise
- New device/location attempting transaction
- Customer may not be aware of compromise yet
- Funds moved to unknown wallet quickly

Example:
- Customer: Established Coinbase user, $50K balance
- Transaction: $45,000 withdrawal to external wallet
- Indicators: New device, different country, 3 AM transaction
- Customer behavior: Different than normal patterns
- Risk Score: 0.85
- Action: HOLD transaction, contact customer immediately, verify intent

SCENARIO 5: ICO / ALTCOIN SCAM
Description: Fraudulent Initial Coin Offering or fake new cryptocurrency.

Pattern:
- Heavy marketing / social media promotion
- Celebrity endorsements (often fake)
- Limited-time opportunity pressure
- White paper is plagiarized or nonsensical
- Anonymous team or fake identities

Example:
- Customer: Wants to invest in "MegaChain ICO"
- Amount: $8,000 via wire to unknown exchange
- Red flags: ICO not registered, team profiles fake, unrealistic promises
- Risk Score: 0.92
- Action: BLOCK, educate customer about ICO fraud

SCENARIO 6: EMPLOYMENT SCAM WITH CRYPTO
Description: Victim offered fake job, receives check, told to buy crypto and send to "employer."

Pattern:
- Check deposit (will bounce in 3-5 days)
- Immediate instruction to purchase crypto
- Urgency: "must do today for equipment purchase"
- Customer believes legitimate employment
- Funds disappear when check bounces

Example:
- Customer: Job seeker, offered "remote assistant" position
- Scenario: Received $3,500 check, told to buy crypto and send $3,000
- Red flags: New employer, immediate crypto request, check from third party
- Risk Score: 0.87
- Action: BLOCK crypto purchase, warn about check fraud, report to FTC

MERCHANT VERIFICATION CHECKLIST

LICENSED EXCHANGES (Lower Risk):
United States: Coinbase, Kraken, Gemini (FinCEN registered, state licensed)
European Union: Bitstamp, Bitpanda (authorized under 5AMLD)
United Kingdom: Crypto.com UK, Gemini UK (FCA registered)
Canada: Coinsquare, NDAX (FINTRAC registered)
Japan: bitFlyer, Coincheck (FSA licensed)

VERIFICATION STEPS:
1. Check regulatory registration in operating jurisdiction
2. Verify domain ownership and age
3. Review online reputation (Reddit, TrustPilot, Better Business Bureau)
4. Confirm customer support channels exist and respond
5. Check for security features (2FA, cold storage, insurance)
6. Review terms of service for red flags

RED FLAGS FOR FAKE EXCHANGES:
- No physical address or registered business
- Website created within last 6 months
- Grammar/spelling errors on website
- No clear ownership or team information
- Promises of guaranteed returns
- No customer support or non-responsive
- Requires payment via gift cards or wire transfers
- Not listed on CoinMarketCap or similar indexes

ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE PROCEDURES

For all crypto transactions triggering this rule:

CUSTOMER INTERVIEW QUESTIONS:
1. "Have you purchased cryptocurrency before?"
2. "What is the purpose of this crypto purchase?"
3. "How did you hear about this exchange/investment?"
4. "Do you understand that crypto transactions cannot be reversed?"
5. "Are you under any pressure to make this purchase?"
6. "Have you verified the legitimacy of the exchange?"
7. "Do you know the recipient of these funds personally?"
8. "Are you making this purchase on behalf of someone else?"
9. "What do you know about cryptocurrency risks?"
10. "Have you been promised specific returns?"

RED FLAG RESPONSES:
- Vague or inconsistent answers
- Mentions third party directing the purchase
- Doesn't understand crypto basics
- Promises of high guaranteed returns
- Romantic partner involved
- "Investment advisor" or "trading expert" mentioned
- Urgency or pressure evident
- Reluctant to answer questions

SOURCE OF FUNDS VERIFICATION:
- Where is the money coming from?
- Recent large deposits (possible scam victim receiving fraudulent check)
- Borrowed money or credit card advances (high risk)
- Retirement account withdrawals (common in elder scams)
- Legitimate employment income and savings (lower risk)

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS:
- Screenshot of intended exchange/investment platform
- Communication history with person/company receiving crypto
- Government-issued ID verification
- Proof of address
- Source of funds documentation
- Written acknowledgment of crypto risks

AUTOMATED BLOCKING CRITERIA

AUTOMATIC BLOCK (No review needed):
1. Unlicensed exchange in strict regulatory jurisdiction
2. Exchange on known scam list
3. Transaction to sanctioned entity (OFAC)
4. Customer explicitly states pressure/scam keywords
5. Multiple fraud indicators score > 0.95

HOLD FOR REVIEW:
1. Risk score 0.80-0.95
2. First-time crypto purchaser with amount > $5,000
3. Elderly customer with amount > $2,000
4. Recent account takeover indicators
5. Failed verification attempts

ALLOW WITH MONITORING:
1. Licensed exchange, established customer, score < 0.70
2. Small amount (< $500), low-risk exchange
3. Customer has successful crypto history
4. Business account with documented crypto operations

CUSTOMER EDUCATION

When blocking crypto transaction, provide:

EDUCATIONAL CONTENT:
- Common crypto scams and how to spot them
- Irreversible nature of crypto transactions
- How to verify exchange legitimacy
- Red flags for investment fraud
- Resources: FTC, FBI IC3, state attorney general

ALTERNATIVE ACTIONS:
- Recommend licensed exchanges (Coinbase, Kraken, etc.)
- Suggest smaller initial purchase to test ($100-$500)
- Encourage verification of recipient
- Wait 24 hours and reconsider (cooling-off period)
- Consult with trusted family member or financial advisor

REPORTING RESOURCES:
- FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3): ic3.gov
- FTC Crypto Scam Reporting: reportfraud.ftc.gov
- State attorney general offices
- Better Business Bureau scam tracker
- Local law enforcement for large losses

PERFORMANCE METRICS

TARGET METRICS:
- Scam Prevention Rate: > 75% (stop fraud before completion)
- False Positive Rate: < 20% (accept higher rate for customer protection)
- Customer Education: 90% receive educational materials when blocked
- Recovery Rate: < 5% (once crypto sent, usually unrecoverable)
- Regulatory Compliance: 100% of SAR requirements met
- Response Time: < 30 minutes for review

MEASURED OUTCOMES:
- Dollar amount of fraud prevented
- Number of customers educated
- SARs filed and investigations supported
- Regulatory compliance audit results
- Customer feedback on education materials

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

LEGITIMATE CRYPTO USE CASES:
- Remittances (lower fees than traditional services)
- International payments (especially in countries with currency controls)
- Store of value / investment (similar to gold)
- DeFi (Decentralized Finance) participation
- NFT purchases (emerging use case)
- Business operations for crypto-related companies

These legitimate uses require different treatment:
- Established pattern of legitimate crypto use
- Business account with documented crypto operations
- Lower risk scores for repeat users
- Documented legitimate purpose

PRIVACY COINS:
Monero (XMR), Zcash (ZEC), Dash present additional risks:
- Enhanced anonymity features
- Harder to trace
- Often used for illicit purposes
- Many exchanges delisting privacy coins
- Additional scrutiny required

DECENTRALIZED EXCHANGES (DEX):
- No central authority
- Harder to regulate
- Less customer protection
- Often no KYC requirements
- Higher fraud risk

COLD WALLETS VS. EXCHANGE CUSTODY:
- Cold wallet transfers (customer holds private keys): higher risk of loss
- Exchange custody (exchange holds keys): some customer protection
- Educational materials should address custody risks

This rule balances legitimate crypto adoption with strong protections against the high fraud risks in the cryptocurrency ecosystem.
